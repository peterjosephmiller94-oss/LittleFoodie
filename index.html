The Gravity Scientists

At 20 months, throwing food isn't malicious—it's physics! Calmly say, "Food stays on the tray." If it happens again, mealtime is paused.

The Appetite Drop-Off

Babies triple their weight in year one. As they approach 2 years, growth slows down. What looks like "starving" is often adequate for them.

Flash Freeze First

Freeze meatballs or patties on a tray for 2 hours before putting them in a bag. They won't stick together, allowing you to pull out exactly what you need!

🧀

The Cheese Glue

If veggies keep falling out of quesadillas or wraps, melt a thin layer of cheese directly onto the tortilla first to act as a secure "glue" for fillings.

🥄

Pre-loaded Spoon Hack

For slippery foods like yogurt or oatmeal, stick the spoon in and let the food solidify slightly in the fridge, giving them a ready-to-grab handle.
