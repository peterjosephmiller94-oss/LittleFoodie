The Little Foodie

20-Month Specific: Psychology & Survival

1

Inside the 20-Month-Old Brain

🍎

The Gravity Scientists

At 20 months, throwing food isn't malicious—it's physics! Calmly say, "Food stays on the tray." If it happens again, mealtime is paused.

📉

The Appetite Drop-Off

Babies triple their weight in year one. As they approach 2 years, growth slows down. What looks like "starving" is often adequate for them.

👑

The Control Freak Phase

Food is one of the only things they have 100% control over. Refusing food is often just them flexing their new autonomy muscle.

Actionable Hacks

The A/B Choice

Give them a controlled choice: "Peas in the red or blue bowl?" They get power; you get them to eat.

The "All Done" Bowl

Put an empty bowl next to their plate for foods they don't want. Gives them a safe action instead of throwing.

Monkey See, Monkey Do

Sit down and eat exaggeratedly. When they see you happily eating, their instinct is to copy you.

2

Presentation Tricks

The "Snackcuterie"

Serve dinner in a cold muffin tin! The novelty of compartments makes food instantly fun.

The "Dip" Trick

Toddlers crave interactivity. Giving them a dip (Greek yogurt) gives them a "job" to do.

Micro-Portions

A full plate looks like a chore. Serve one single bite at a time, then offer "More?!"

3

Emergency Swaps

Out of Eggs?

The Flax Egg

Mix 1 tbsp ground flax with 3 tbsp warm water. Let sit 5 mins. Use as 1:1 replacement.

Out of Breadcrumbs?

The Oat Swap

Pulse rolled oats in a blender until coarse. Use exactly as breadcrumbs.

Out of Flour?

The Veggie Binder

Substitute with mashed potato in fritters. Acts as a nutrient-dense glue.

Out of Milk?

The Broth Trick

If a recipe calls for milk for moisture, substitute unsalted chicken/veg broth.

      Week 1 Recipes

      1. Turkey/Beef Meatballs

      Ingredients

      • • 500g Ground turkey or lean beef
      • • 1 Large egg (lightly beaten)
      • • 50g Panko or regular breadcrumbs
      • • 120g Zucchini (finely grated and squeezed dry)
      • • 1 tsp Onion powder & 1/2 tsp Garlic powder
      • • Pinch of black pepper

      Instructions

      1. 1. Prep Veggies: Finely grate the zucchini. Place it in a clean kitchen towel and wring out as much moisture as humanly possible to prevent soggy meatballs.
      2. 2. Mix: In a large bowl, combine the ground meat, beaten egg, dry zucchini, breadcrumbs, and spices. Mix gently with your hands just until combined.
      3. 3. Shape: Scoop about 1 tablespoon of the mixture and gently roll it into 3cm balls. Place them on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
      4. 4. Bake: Bake in a preheated oven at 190°C (375°F) for 18-20 minutes, or until cooked through and slightly golden on the outside. Let cool before serving.

      2. Salmon Fish Fingers

      Ingredients

      • • 300g Skinless salmon fillet (bones removed)
      • • 1 Large egg (beaten)
      • • 30g All-purpose flour
      • • 50g Panko breadcrumbs
      • • 1/2 tsp Paprika
      • • Olive oil spray

      Instructions

      1. 1. Prep Salmon: Carefully check the salmon for any pin bones. Cut the fillet into toddler-friendly strips, about 2cm wide and 6cm long.
      2. 2. Setup Station: Prepare three shallow bowls: one with flour, one with the beaten egg, and one with panko mixed with paprika.
      3. 3. Dredge: Coat each salmon strip lightly in flour, dip it into the egg (letting excess drip off), and finally press it firmly into the panko mixture until fully coated.
      4. 4. Bake: Place the strips on a greased baking tray, spray lightly with olive oil, and bake at 200°C (400°F) for 12-15 minutes until the fish flakes easily and the crust is golden.

      3. Black Bean Quesadillas

      Ingredients

      • • 1 Whole wheat or flour tortilla (8-inch)
      • • 50g Canned black beans (rinsed and drained well)
      • • 30g Shredded mild cheddar or Monterey Jack cheese
      • • 1 tsp Olive oil or butter
      • • Optional: 1 tbsp mild salsa or sweetcorn

      Instructions

      1. 1. Mash Beans: In a small bowl, use a fork to thoroughly mash the black beans until they form a thick, spreadable paste. This prevents choking hazards and helps the quesadilla stick together.
      2. 2. Assemble: Spread the mashed black beans evenly over one half of the tortilla. Top with the shredded cheese and optional sweetcorn, then fold the empty half over the top.
      3. 3. Cook: Heat the olive oil or butter in a skillet over medium-low heat. Place the folded quesadilla in the pan.
      4. 4. Toast: Cook for 2-3 minutes until the bottom is golden brown and crispy. Carefully flip and cook the other side for another 2 minutes until the cheese is completely melted. Cut into small triangles or strips.

      4. Lentil Sweet Potato Cups

      Ingredients

      • • 200g Cooked brown or green lentils (canned is fine, rinsed)
      • • 100g Finely diced onion and carrots
      • • 400g Sweet potatoes (peeled and cubed)
      • • 50ml Low-sodium vegetable broth
      • • 1 tbsp Olive oil
      • • Pinch of dried thyme

      Instructions

      1. 1. Mash Potato: Boil the sweet potato cubes until very tender (about 15 mins). Drain well and mash smoothly. Set aside to cool slightly.
      2. 2. Sauté Veggies: Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the finely diced onions and carrots, cooking for 5-7 minutes until completely soft and translucent.
      3. 3. Simmer Base: Stir the cooked lentils, vegetable broth, and thyme into the veggie mixture. Simmer on low heat for 3-5 minutes until most of the liquid has absorbed or evaporated.
      4. 4. Assemble: Grease a muffin tin. Spoon the lentil mixture into each cup, filling them about 2/3 full. Top each with a generous dollop of the sweet potato mash, smoothing it to the edges.
      5. 5. Bake: Bake at 180°C (350°F) for 15-20 minutes until the edges are slightly firm. Let them cool in the tin for 10 minutes so they hold their shape before removing.

      5. Chicken & Veggie Fritters

      Ingredients

      • • 150g Cooked and finely shredded chicken breast
      • • 120g Zucchini (grated and moisture squeezed out)
      • • 1 Large egg
      • • 40g All-purpose flour or oat flour
      • • 20g Grated parmesan cheese
      • • 1 tbsp Olive oil for frying

      Instructions

      1. 1. Prep Ingredients: Ensure the shredded chicken is chopped very fine to avoid stringy textures. Grate the zucchini and wring it out tightly in a towel to remove excess water.
      2. 2. Create Batter: In a medium bowl, whisk the egg. Add the dry zucchini, finely chopped chicken, and parmesan cheese. Mix well. Gently fold in the flour until just combined into a thick batter. Let it rest for 2-3 minutes.
      3. 3. Portion: Heat olive oil in a large non-stick skillet over medium heat. Drop heaped tablespoons of the mixture into the pan, using the back of the spoon to flatten them into 1cm thick patties.
      4. 4. Pan-Fry: Cook for 3-4 minutes on the first side until golden brown and crispy. Carefully flip and cook for another 3 minutes on the other side. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

      6. Homemade Pizza Strips

      Ingredients

      • • 2 Whole wheat pita breads or mini naan breads
      • • 3-4 tbsp Low-sodium pizza or pasta sauce
      • • 50g Low-moisture mozzarella cheese (shredded)
      • • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
      • • Optional: finely chopped bell peppers or spinach

      Instructions

      1. 1. Prep Bases: Place the pita breads flat on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. If they have a pocket, do not split them; use them whole for a sturdier base.
      2. 2. Add Toppings: Spread 1.5 to 2 tablespoons of pizza sauce evenly over each pita, going almost to the edges. Sprinkle the Italian seasoning over the sauce, followed by an even layer of shredded mozzarella. Add any optional, finely chopped veggies on top.
      3. 3. Bake: Place in a preheated oven at 200°C (400°F) and bake for 8-10 minutes. The edges of the pita should be crispy and the cheese should be melted and lightly bubbling.
      4. 4. Serve: Remove from the oven and let cool for 3-5 minutes to prevent mouth burns. Use a pizza cutter or sharp knife to slice the circular pizzas into long, rectangular strips (about 2cm wide) which are much easier for 20-month-old hands to grip than wide wedges.

      7. Mild Coconut Chicken Curry

      Ingredients

      • • 450g Boneless skinless chicken thighs (cut into 1cm bite-sized cubes)
      • • 400ml Full-fat coconut milk (canned)
      • • 1 tbsp Mild, salt-free curry powder
      • • 200g Sweet potato (peeled and diced very small)
      • • 1/2 cup Frozen peas
      • • 1 tbsp Coconut or olive oil

      Instructions

      1. 1. Sauté Chicken: Heat the oil in a large, deep skillet over medium-high heat. Add the diced chicken and cook for 4-5 minutes until browned on the outside.
      2. 2. Spice & Simmer: Lower the heat to medium. Stir in the mild curry powder and cook for 1 minute until fragrant. Pour in the coconut milk and add the finely diced sweet potatoes. Stir well to combine.
      3. 3. Cook Through: Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer, cover, and cook for 15-20 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are fork-tender and the chicken is fully cooked. Stir in the frozen peas during the last 3 minutes of cooking.
      4. 4. Thicken & Serve: To make the sauce easier for a toddler to eat with a spoon, take a fork and mash some of the soft sweet potato pieces directly into the sauce to naturally thicken it. Let it cool significantly before serving over soft rice.

      Week 2 Recipes

      8. Mini Meatloaf Muffins

      Ingredients

      • • 500g Lean ground beef
      • • 1 Small apple (peeled and finely grated)
      • • 1 Medium carrot (peeled and finely grated)
      • • 1 Large egg
      • • 40g Quick oats (or pulverized rolled oats)
      • • 1 tsp Tomato paste

      Instructions

      1. 1. Prep Produce: Peel and finely grate the apple and carrot. Squeeze out excess liquid from the grated apple so it doesn't make the mixture too wet.
      2. 2. Mix: In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, grated apple, grated carrot, egg, oats, and tomato paste. Use your hands to gently mix everything until evenly distributed, being careful not to overwork the meat.
      3. 3. Shape: Lightly grease a 12-cup muffin tin. Divide the meat mixture evenly among the cups, pressing it down gently so the top is flat.
      4. 4. Bake: Bake in a preheated oven at 190°C (375°F) for 20-25 minutes. The internal temperature should reach 71°C (160°F). Let the muffins rest in the tin for 5 minutes before popping them out to ensure they hold their shape.

      9. Pesto Pea Spirals

      Ingredients

      • • 200g Fusilli or rotini pasta (the spirals hold sauce best)
      • • 100g Frozen sweet peas
      • • 2 tbsp High-quality basil pesto (check for no added sugar)
      • • 2 tbsp Plain full-fat Greek yogurt
      • • 1 tsp Olive oil
      • • Optional: a sprinkle of parmesan cheese

      Instructions

      1. 1. Boil Pasta: Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a rolling boil. Add the fusilli pasta and cook according to the package directions, adding an extra 1-2 minutes so it's slightly past al dente and very soft for toddler chewing.
      2. 2. Add Peas: Exactly 2 minutes before the pasta is done, pour the frozen peas directly into the boiling pasta water to blanch them.
      3. 3. Drain: Drain the pasta and peas thoroughly. Do not rinse them, as the natural starches will help the sauce stick. Return them to the warm (but off-heat) pot.
      4. 4. Coat & Serve: Stir in the olive oil, basil pesto, and Greek yogurt. Mix vigorously until the pasta is completely coated in a creamy green sauce. Let cool to a safe temperature and sprinkle with parmesan if using.

      10. White Bean & Spinach Patties

      Ingredients

      • • 1 Can (400g) Cannellini or Great Northern beans (rinsed and drained)
      • • 1 Large handful fresh spinach (finely chopped)
      • • 1 Large egg
      • • 40g Panko breadcrumbs
      • • 1/4 tsp Garlic powder
      • • 1 tbsp Olive oil for cooking

      Instructions

      1. 1. Prep Beans: Rinse the beans extremely well to remove excess sodium. Place them in a medium bowl and use a potato masher or fork to mash them until mostly smooth, leaving a few chunks for texture.
      2. 2. Combine: Finely chop the spinach to avoid any stringy choking hazards. Add the chopped spinach, egg, breadcrumbs, and garlic powder to the mashed beans. Stir until a cohesive, slightly sticky dough forms.
      3. 3. Form Patties: Divide the mixture into 6-8 equal portions. Roll them into balls and flatten them into patties about 1.5cm thick. If the mixture is too wet, add an extra tablespoon of breadcrumbs.
      4. 4. Cook: Heat olive oil in a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Carefully place the patties in the pan and cook for 3-4 minutes per side until a golden, crispy crust forms. Let cool completely before serving.

      11. Hidden Veggie Mac & Cheese

      Ingredients

      • • 200g Elbow macaroni or small shell pasta
      • • 150g Smooth butternut squash or sweet potato puree (canned or homemade)
      • • 100g Sharp cheddar cheese (freshly grated melts best)
      • • 60ml Whole milk
      • • 1 tsp Unsalted butter

      Instructions

      1. 1. Cook Pasta: Bring a pot of water to a boil and cook the macaroni for 2 minutes longer than the package directions indicate to ensure it is extremely soft and easy for a toddler to manage. Drain well.
      2. 2. Create Veggie Base: In the same pot (now empty and off the heat), melt the butter. Whisk in the butternut squash puree and the whole milk until smooth. Turn the heat onto the lowest possible setting.
      3. 3. Melt Cheese: Gradually sprinkle in the grated cheddar cheese, stirring constantly in a figure-eight motion until the cheese is completely melted and the sauce is glossy and smooth. Do not let it boil.
      4. 4. Combine: Fold the cooked macaroni back into the pot with the cheese sauce. Stir gently until every noodle is coated. If the sauce is too thick, add another splash of milk.

      12. Deconstructed Tacos

      Ingredients

      • • 100g Leftover cooked ground beef (from meatloaf prep)
      • • 50g Canned black beans (rinsed well)
      • • 1 Small soft flour tortilla
      • • 30g Mild cheddar cheese (shredded)
      • • 2 tbsp Plain full-fat Greek yogurt (as a sour cream swap)
      • • 1/4 Avocado (mashed)

      Instructions

      1. 1. Prep Components: Warm the leftover ground beef slightly. Take the black beans and gently smash them flat with a fork to remove the round, rolling choking hazard. Mash the avocado until completely smooth.
      2. 2. Cut Tortilla: Using clean kitchen scissors or a knife, cut the soft flour tortilla into finger-width strips. This makes it easier for 20-month-olds to practice holding and biting.
      3. 3. The "Dip" Setup: Place the Greek yogurt and mashed avocado in two small, separate silicone muffin liners or tiny bowls to encourage dipping, which toddlers love.
      4. 4. Plate: Use a divided toddler plate. Place the beef in one section, the smashed beans and cheese in another, the tortilla strips in a third, and the dips in the center. Do NOT assemble the taco, as mixed textures can be overwhelming.

      13. Banana Oat Pancakes

      Ingredients

      • • 1 Very ripe, spotty banana (the browner, the sweeter)
      • • 1 Large egg
      • • 40g Rolled oats
      • • 1/4 tsp Cinnamon
      • • 1/2 tsp Vanilla extract
      • • 1 tsp Butter or coconut oil (for the pan)

      Instructions

      1. 1. Blend Batter: Place the rolled oats in a blender and pulse a few times until they form a coarse flour. Add the ripe banana, egg, cinnamon, and vanilla extract. Blend on high until completely smooth and bubbly. Let the batter sit for 3 minutes to thicken.
      2. 2. Heat Pan: Melt the butter or coconut oil in a large non-stick skillet over medium-low heat. It's crucial not to have the heat too high, or the outside will burn before the inside cooks.
      3. 3. Pour: Pour small, silver-dollar-sized circles of batter into the pan (about 1.5 tablespoons each). Small pancakes are easier to flip and perfect for toddler hands.
      4. 4. Cook & Flip: Cook for 2-3 minutes until you see tiny bubbles forming on the surface and the edges look set. Gently flip and cook for another 1-2 minutes until golden brown. Cool completely before serving.

      14. Tomato Soup & Cheese Strips

      Ingredients

      • • 150ml Low-sodium, creamy tomato soup (check labels for no added sugar)
      • • 1 Slice whole wheat or sourdough bread
      • • 1 Slice mild cheddar or provolone cheese
      • • 1 tsp Butter (softened)
      • • 1 tbsp Heavy cream or whole milk (optional)

      Instructions

      1. 1. Prepare Soup: Pour the tomato soup into a small saucepan. Heat over low heat until just warm to the touch—never hot, as toddler mouths are very sensitive to temperature. Stir in a tablespoon of cream or milk to cool it down and add extra calories.
      2. 2. Make Grilled Cheese: Heat a small skillet over medium heat. Butter one side of the bread slice. Place it butter-side down in the skillet, add the cheese slice, and if using half a slice of bread folded, fold it over. Cook until the bread is golden and the cheese is melted.
      3. 3. Cut for Dunking: Remove the grilled cheese from the pan and let it cool slightly. Using a sharp knife, cut the sandwich into long, thin strips (about 1.5cm wide).
      4. 4. Serve: Serve the lukewarm soup in a shallow bowl or a mug with handles alongside the cheese strips, demonstrating to the toddler how to "dunk" the strips into the soup for interactive eating.

      Final Prep & Nutrition Hacks

      The Dry Zucchini Rule

      Grate it, wring it out until dry to prevent mushy meatballs.

      The Freezer Secret

      Freeze items flat on a tray for 2 hours *before* bagging.

      The Iron Combo

      Pair plant iron (lentils) with Vit C (berries) for absorption.
