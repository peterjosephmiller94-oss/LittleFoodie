Inside the 20-Month-Old Brain

🍎

The Gravity Scientists

At 20 months, throwing food isn't malicious—it's physics! Calmly say, "Food stays on the tray." If it happens again, mealtime is paused.

📉

The Appetite Drop-Off

Babies triple their weight in year one. As they approach 2 years, growth slows down. What looks like "starving" is often adequate for them.

👑

The Control Freak Phase

Food is one of the only things they have 100% control over. Refusing food is often just them flexing their new autonomy muscle.

      Week 1 Recipes

      1. Turkey/Beef Meatballs

      Ingredients

      • • 500g Ground meat
      • • 1 Large egg
      • • 50g Breadcrumbs
      • • 120g Zucchini (dry)

      Instructions

      1. 1. Prepare Zucchini: Grate the zucchini finely. Place it in a clean kitchen towel and wring it out with as much force as possible. It must be bone-dry to prevent the meatballs from becoming mushy.
      2. 2. Combine Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground meat, egg, breadcrumbs, and the dried zucchini. Use your hands to gently mix until just combined—do not overwork the meat or it will become tough for little jaws.
      3. 3. Portion & Roll: Scoop approximately 1 tablespoon of mixture and roll into 3cm balls. This size is perfect for "micro-portions" and easy for a toddler to pick up.
      4. 4. Bake: Arrange on a parchment-lined tray. Bake at 190°C (375°F) for 18-20 minutes. Ensure the internal temperature reaches 74°C (165°F).

      2. Salmon Fish Fingers

      Ingredients

      • • 300g Salmon Fillet
      • • 1 Egg
      • • 30g Flour
      • • 60g Panko Breadcrumbs

      Instructions

      1. 1. Slice the Salmon: Remove any skin and carefully feel for bones. Slice the salmon into fingers roughly 2cm wide and 6cm long. This "finger" shape is easiest for a 20-month-old's palmar grasp.
      2. 2. Set Up Station: Prepare three shallow bowls: one with flour, one with the beaten egg, and one with panko.
      3. 3. Bread the Fish: Dip each piece into flour (shake off excess), then egg, then roll in panko until fully coated. Press the panko in firmly so it stays crunchy.
      4. 4. Bake: Place on a baking sheet and lightly spray with olive oil. Bake at 200°C (400°F) for 12-15 minutes until the salmon flakes easily and the coating is golden.

      3. Black Bean Quesadillas

      Ingredients

      • • 1 Flour Tortilla
      • • 50g Canned Black beans (rinsed)
      • • 30g Mild Cheddar

      Instructions

      1. 1. Safety Smash: Whole beans can be a choking hazard. Place rinsed beans on a plate and smash them flat with the back of a fork. This also helps them stick inside the tortilla!
      2. 2. Layering: Spread the smashed beans over half of the tortilla. Sprinkle the cheese evenly over the beans. Fold the tortilla in half.
      3. 3. Toasting: Heat a non-stick skillet over medium-low heat. Toast the quesadilla for 2 minutes per side until the cheese is melted and acts as a "glue" for the beans.
      4. 4. Slice: Let it cool for 3 minutes (molten cheese is a burn risk). Slice into thin triangles or strips.

      4. Lentil Sweet Potato Cups

      Ingredients

      • • 200g Cooked lentils
      • • 100g Onion/Carrot (minced)
      • • 400g Sweet Potato (boiled)
      • • 50ml Vegetable Broth

      Instructions

      1. 1. Sauté the Base: In a pan with a splash of oil, cook the finely minced onions and carrots until very soft (about 8 minutes). Texture is key—crunchy onions are often rejected at this age.
      2. 2. Combine & Simmer: Stir in the cooked lentils and broth. Simmer on low heat until the liquid is mostly gone and the mixture is thick and scoopable.
      3. 3. Prepare the Mash: Mash the boiled sweet potatoes until completely smooth. Add a tiny bit of butter or olive oil if needed for a silky texture.
      4. 4. Assemble & Bake: Grease a muffin tin. Fill each cup 2/3 with the lentil mixture. Spoon the sweet potato mash on top. Bake at 180°C (350°F) for 15 minutes to set. These are great for practicing with a fork!

      5. Chicken & Veggie Fritters

      Ingredients

      • • 150g Shredded chicken
      • • 120g Zucchini (dry)
      • • 1 Large egg
      • • 40g Flour

      Instructions

      1. 1. Prep the Chicken: Ensure the chicken is finely shredded or chopped into tiny bits. Long strands can be hard for toddlers to manage.
      2. 2. Create the Batter: In a bowl, whisk the egg. Add the shredded chicken, bone-dry zucchini, and flour. Mix until you have a thick, sticky batter. Let it sit for 2 minutes to allow the flour to hydrate.
      3. 3. Pan-Fry: Heat a lightly oiled skillet over medium heat. Drop heaping tablespoons of batter into the pan and flatten them into discs with a spatula.
      4. 4. Cook: Fry for 3-4 minutes per side until golden brown and cooked through. Serve with a dollop of Greek yogurt as a "dip" to encourage interaction.

      6. Homemade Pizza Strips

      Ingredients

      • • 2 Whole-wheat Pitas
      • • 4 tbsp Low-sodium sauce
      • • 50g Mozzarella

      Instructions

      1. 1. Base Prep: Using pita bread instead of dough makes for a thinner, easier-to-chew crust. Spread a thin layer of sauce on the pita, leaving a small border for the "crust."
      2. 2. Cheese & Veggie: Sprinkle mozzarella evenly. If you have extra finely grated carrots or spinach, you can hide them under the cheese here.
      3. 3. Bake: Bake at 200°C (400°F) for 8-10 minutes until the cheese is bubbling and just starting to brown.
      4. 4. Final Cut: Let cool completely. Use a pizza cutter to slice the rounds into 2cm wide rectangles (strips). These "fries" shapes are much more toddler-friendly than traditional wedges.

      7. Mild Coconut Chicken Curry

      Ingredients

      • • 450g Chicken (1cm cubes)
      • • 400ml Full-fat coconut milk
      • • 1 tbsp Mild curry powder
      • • 200g Sweet potato (diced)

      Instructions

      1. 1. Searing: Dice the chicken into very small, uniform cubes (1cm). In a deep pan, brown the chicken slightly in olive oil. Add the mild curry powder and stir for 30 seconds until fragrant.
      2. 2. Liquid & Veg: Pour in the coconut milk and add the diced sweet potatoes. Ensure the potatoes are also cut small so they soften quickly.
      3. 3. Simmer: Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low. Simmer for 20-25 minutes until the chicken is tender and potatoes are soft.
      4. 4. Thicken & Serve: Use a fork to mash some of the sweet potatoes directly into the sauce. This naturally thickens the curry, making it cling to a spoon better for "spoon practice."

      Week 2 Recipes

      8. Mini Meatloaf Muffins

      Ingredients

      • • 500g Ground Beef
      • • 1 Grated Apple & Carrot
      • • 1 Egg
      • • 40g Rolled Oats

      Instructions

      1. 1. The Grate Secret: Fine-grate the apple and carrot. The apple provides natural sweetness and incredible moisture, ensuring the meatloaf isn't dry or crumbly for the toddler.
      2. 2. Mix: Combine the beef, grated veg/fruit, egg, and oats. Oats are used here instead of breadcrumbs for extra fiber and a softer final texture.
      3. 3. Shape: Press the mixture into a greased muffin tin. Fill them to the top; they will shrink slightly as they cook.
      4. 4. Bake: Bake at 190°C (375°F) for 20-25 minutes. Let them sit for 5 minutes before removing. Serve as "micro-portions" to avoid overwhelming the plate.

      9. Pesto Pea Spirals

      Ingredients

      • • 200g Fusilli Pasta
      • • 100g Frozen Peas
      • • 2 tbsp Basil Pesto
      • • 1 tbsp Greek Yogurt

      Instructions

      1. 1. Boil: Cook the fusilli in boiling water. For 20-month olds, cook it 1-2 minutes *past* the al dente stage to ensure it's soft enough for their molars.
      2. 2. Add Peas: Throw the frozen peas into the pasta water during the last 2-3 minutes of cooking. Drain everything together.
      3. 3. The Creamy Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk the pesto with the Greek yogurt. This adds protein and tempers the strong flavor of the pesto, making it more palatable for toddlers.
      4. 4. Toss: Mix the pasta, peas, and sauce. The spirals are excellent for "fork practice" as they are easy to stab!

      10. White Bean & Spinach Patties

      Ingredients

      • • 1 Can White Beans
      • • 1 Handful Spinach (chopped)
      • • 1 Egg
      • • 40g Breadcrumbs

      Instructions

      1. 1. Mash the Beans: Drain and rinse the beans. Place them in a bowl and mash them with a fork until mostly smooth but with a few small chunks for texture.
      2. 2. Fold in Greens: Finely chop the fresh spinach (steam it briefly first if your toddler is picky about raw greens). Mix the spinach, egg, and breadcrumbs into the beans.
      3. 3. Chill: If you have time, chill the mixture for 10 minutes. This makes the patties easier to handle. Form into small, toddler-sized patties.
      4. 4. Cook: Pan-fry in a little oil for 3-4 minutes per side until a crust forms. These are high in iron—pair with a slice of orange for vitamin C absorption!

      11. Hidden Veggie Mac & Cheese

      Ingredients

      • • 200g Macaroni
      • • 150g Squash/Pumpkin Puree
      • • 100g Grated Cheddar
      • • 50ml Milk

      Instructions

      1. 1. Boil: Cook the macaroni until very soft. Drain, but reserve a small amount of the pasta water.
      2. 2. Create the "Gold" Sauce: In the same warm pot, combine the squash puree, grated cheddar, and milk. Stir over low heat until the cheese melts and blends into a vibrant orange, silky sauce.
      3. 3. Combine: Return the pasta to the pot and toss to coat. If it's too thick, add a splash of the reserved pasta water.
      4. 4. Serve: Serve in a muffin tin (Snackcuterie style) with peas or diced carrots in the other compartments to keep things interactive.

      12. Deconstructed Tacos

      Ingredients

      • • Ground Beef (cooked)
      • • Black Beans (smashed)
      • • Mild Cheese
      • • Whole Wheat Tortilla

      Instructions

      1. 1. Prep Ingredients: Ensure the beef is crumbled into very small pieces. Smash the black beans with a fork.
      2. 2. Soften the Tortilla: Warm the tortilla briefly so it's soft, then use a pizza cutter to slice it into long, 1cm wide "ribbons."
      3. 3. Plate Construction: Do not build the taco. Place the beef, the bean mash, the cheese, and the tortilla strips in separate piles on a divided plate.
      4. 4. Interactive Eating: This "deconstructed" style reduces overwhelm and lets the toddler explore each texture individually—perfect for the "Control Freak Phase."

      13. Banana Oat Pancakes

      Ingredients

      • • 1 Very Ripe Banana
      • • 1 Large Egg
      • • 40g Rolled Oats (blitzed)

      Instructions

      1. 1. Blend to Smooth: Place the banana, egg, and oats in a blender. Blitz until completely smooth. Using oats instead of flour creates a much softer pancake that is safer for early chewers.
      2. 2. Low & Slow: Heat a non-stick pan over medium-low heat with a tiny bit of butter. These pancakes have no flour, so they burn easily if the heat is too high.
      3. 3. Pouring: Pour small, "dollar-sized" circles (about 5cm wide). This makes them easier for small hands to pick up.
      4. 4. The Flip: Wait for small bubbles to form on the surface, then gently flip. Cook for 1 more minute. Serve with a "dip" of Greek yogurt.

      14. Tomato Soup & Cheese Strips

      Ingredients

      • • Low-Sodium Tomato Soup
      • • 1 slice Bread
      • • 1 slice Cheddar

      Instructions

      1. 1. Soup Temp: Warm the tomato soup until it is just above room temperature. Never serve "hot" to a toddler. If it's too thin, stir in a spoonful of yogurt to make it more nutrient-dense.
      2. 2. Grilled Cheese: Make a simple grilled cheese sandwich. Use a tiny bit of butter and keep the crusts soft.
      3. 3. Soldiers: Use a knife to cut the sandwich into long "soldiers" (strips about 1.5cm wide).
      4. 4. Interaction: Demonstrate the "dunking" action. This provides a "job" for the toddler and makes the meal interactive rather than a chore.

      Final Prep & Nutrition Hacks

      The Dry Zucchini Rule

      Grate it, wring it out until dry to prevent mushy meatballs.

      The Freezer Secret

      Freeze items flat on a tray for 2 hours *before* bagging.

      The Iron Combo

      Pair plant iron (lentils) with Vit C (berries) for absorption.
