Inside the 20-Month-Old Brain

🍎

The Gravity Scientists

At 20 months, throwing food isn't malicious—it's physics! Calmly say, "Food stays on the tray." If it happens again, mealtime is paused.

📉

The Appetite Drop-Off

Babies triple their weight in year one. As they approach 2 years, growth slows down. What looks like "starving" is often adequate for them.

👑

The Control Freak Phase

Food is one of the only things they have 100% control over. Refusing food is often just them flexing their new autonomy muscle.

🥦

Food Chaining

Introduce challenging foods by linking them to accepted ones. If they like french fries, try sweet potato fries next, then roasted carrots.

🍓

The Sweet Bridge

Pairing a difficult vegetable with a familiar, highly desired fruit (like serving spinach patties next to strawberries) makes the entire plate feel "safe."

⚖️

Division of Responsibility

You decide what, when, and where they eat. They decide if and how much. This instantly removes major mealtime power struggles!

The Dry Zucchini Rule

Whenever adding grated veggies to meatballs or fritters, wring them out in a towel until bone-dry. It prevents the mix from falling apart into mush!

🧊

The Freezer Secret

Freeze cooked items (like fritters or patties) flat on a baking tray for 2 hours *before* bagging them. They won't stick together this way.

🩸

The Iron Combo

Plant-based iron (lentils, beans) is hard for bodies to absorb. Always pair them with Vitamin C (strawberries, tomatoes, bell peppers) to boost absorption by up to 300%!

Week 1 Recipes

1. Turkey/Beef Meatballs

Prep: 15m | Cook: 20m

Ingredients

  • • 500g Ground meat
  • • 1 Large egg
  • • 50g Breadcrumbs
  • • 120g Zucchini (dry)

Instructions

  1. 1. Prepare Zucchini: Grate the zucchini finely. Wring it out in a towel until bone-dry to prevent mushy textures.
  2. 2. Combine Ingredients: Mix meat, egg, breadcrumbs, and zucchini gently. Do not overwork, allowing the meat to stay tender for experienced chompers.
  3. 3. Portion & Roll: Roll into slightly larger 4cm balls. This size is perfect for advanced toddlers to practice stabbing effectively with a fork without the ball crumbling.
  4. 4. Bake: Bake at 190°C (375°F) for 18-20 minutes until a firm outer crust develops for sensory feedback.
